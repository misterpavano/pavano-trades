Volatility
VIX: 23.57
+2.13 (+9.93%)  |  Fear elevated

VIX spiked nearly 10% today, closing at 23.57. This is a meaningful fear reading — comfortably into the "elevated uncertainty" zone, though not yet in panic territory (30+). A VIX at 23 means the market is pricing in roughly 1.4% daily moves in the S&P going forward. Options premiums are expensive right now on both sides.

📌 What This Means: Selling premium is attractive when VIX is elevated, buying it is expensive. If you're playing 0DTE or short-dated options, be aware of vol crush risk — if geopolitical situation de-escalates, VIX drops fast and long options lose value even if direction is right.

TLT (long-duration Treasury ETF) barely moved today despite the equity selloff — a slight negative. In classic risk-off events, bonds rally as investors seek safety. The muted bond reaction suggests markets are not convinced this is a pure flight-to-safety trade. Inflation fears from higher oil are keeping Treasury buyers cautious.

📌 What This Means: Bonds aren't rallying with stocks falling — that's a warning sign. It means the market sees this as potentially inflationary (oil shock), not just a growth scare. That combination is harder for the Fed to navigate and harder for equities to recover from quickly.
