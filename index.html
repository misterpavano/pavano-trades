Understanding Implied Volatility Around Earnings
Vertex AI Search, TipRanks.com | April 16, 2026
Implied volatility is a crucial concept in options trading, representing the market's expectation of how much a stock's price will move in the future. Around earnings announcements, implied volatility often rises significantly as traders anticipate large price swings, making options more expensive. After the earnings report, if the actual price movement is less than what was implied, implied volatility typically drops, a phenomenon known as 'volatility crush,' which can negatively impact option prices.
