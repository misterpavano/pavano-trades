Macro Move
$5.7 Trillion Expires Today
Quadruple Witching • Third Friday of March • Stock futures + index futures + stock options + index options all expire simultaneously • Largest derivatives event of Q1

Institutional Desks Are Rolling Trillions Today — Volatility Is a Feature, Not a Bug

Quadruple Witching happens four times a year and today is one of them. $5.7 trillion in derivatives expire simultaneously: stock index futures, stock index options, single-stock options, and single-stock futures. What this actually means for money flow: every major institutional desk must either roll their positions to June expiry or let them expire. That roll process creates enormous volume, directional pressure, and intraday reversals that have nothing to do with fundamentals. The futures were already being suppressed by $5.7T in expiration mechanics before a single economic print hit the tape.

📌 What This Means: Do not fight the tape in the first hour. The open is a witching-driven chaos window. By 10:30-11 AM ET, desks finish rolling and the real price discovery begins. Anything that happens at the bell is noise. Real positioning is revealed in the second half of the session.

The sectors that win in this environment are energy (oil), financials with floating-rate assets, and value. Watch where the rotation is going, not where it came from.
